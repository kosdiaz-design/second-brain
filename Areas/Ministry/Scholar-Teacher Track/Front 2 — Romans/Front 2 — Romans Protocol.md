# FRONT 2 — ROMANS DEEP-STUDY PROTOCOL
### Converting intake into interpretive skill. One book, to the studs.

> [!info] Campaign home
> Part of the **[[Ministry]]** area · Scholar-Teacher Track · Front 2
> Weekly worksheets: [[Week 01 — Romans 1.1-17]]

---

## 1. MISSION

Move from *reader* of Romans to *handler* of Romans. By Day 90 you will be able to teach the book verse-by-verse, defend its hard passages, and trace its argument from memory. This is not a reading plan — it is weapons training on a single text.

> *"Do your best to present yourself to God as one approved, a worker who has no need to be ashamed, rightly handling the word of truth."* — 2 Timothy 2:15

---

## 2. THE GOVERNOR (non-negotiable order of operations)

> *"Ezra had set his heart to **study** the Law of the LORD, and to **do it**, and to **teach** his statutes."* — Ezra 7:10

Study → Obey → Teach. In that sequence. The middle term is the one that makes the teaching trustworthy. Each week you do not advance to the next sector until you can name how the current passage commands your own life. Knowledge that outruns obedience is the standard casualty in this calling.

---

## 3. END-STATE (graduation criteria — measurable)

You have completed Front 2 when you can do all five without notes:

- [ ] State the argument of the **whole book** in 5 minutes, movement by movement.
- [ ] Produce a **one-page argument map** of Romans 1–16 from memory.
- [ ] **Teach all sixteen chapters** to your men's study across the campaign.
- [ ] **Defend the three hard fronts**: the identity of the "I" in 7:14–25, sovereign election in ch. 9, submission to government in 13:1–7.
- [ ] **Write the "big idea"** of each of the seven movements in one sentence each.

## 4. THE ARMORY (required resources, tiered)

| Tier | Resource | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Method manual | Fee & Stuart, *How to Read the Bible for All Its Worth* | Hermeneutics — work it like a manual, not a read |
| Accessible commentary | John Stott, *The Message of Romans* (BST) | Pastoral, readable, theologically rich |
| Technical commentary | Douglas Moo, *Romans* (NICNT) | The gold standard — wrestle the hard verses here |
| Tracing / diagramming | Biblearc.com (phrasing/arcing tool) | Map the logical flow visually |
| Lexicon & cross-refs | Logos / Accordance, or Blue Letter Bible (free) | Word studies, original-language touchpoints |
| Translations | ESV (primary) + NASB + NET (notes) | Compare for observation; NET notes are a free seminary |

> [!warning] Discipline shift
> You have been reading *wide and fast*. The scholar is made reading *narrow and slow*. Resist the urge to cover ground.

---

## 5. THE WEEKLY BATTLE RHYTHM (the engine — repeat every week)

Six working days, Sunday Sabbath. Each day has one job. Do not skip Day 1–2 to rush to commentary; the discipline of seeing the text *before* the scholars is the whole point.

| Day | Phase | The single job |
|-----|-------|----------------|
| **MON** | **RECON** | Read the week's passage 3x in 3 translations. No commentary. Mark repeated words, contrasts, and every connective — *therefore, but, so that, because*. The logic lives in the connectives. |
| **TUE** | **STRUCTURE** | Diagram the argument. Separate main clauses from subordinate. Map: claim → support → conclusion. |
| **WED** | **INTERROGATION** | Pick 2–3 key terms. Trace them in the lexicon and run their cross-references. |
| **THU** | **COUNSEL** | Now read the commentary. Compare your interpretation to Stott/Moo. Resolve the hard verses; note *why* you were wrong. |
| **FRI** | **SYNTHESIS** | Write the passage's argument in your own words — one paragraph. State the **big idea** in one sentence. |
| **SAT** | **DEPLOYMENT** | Build the teaching outline. Prepare to teach it — or teach it live at the men's study. |
| **SUN** | **SABBATH** | Rest. Worship. No study. The Word you've handled all week works on you while you cease from labor. |

## 7. CAPSTONE (Week 13 — the final exam)

1. Write the **one-page argument map** of all sixteen chapters from memory, then check it.
2. **Teach the book's overview** — the 5-minute flyover — to your men's study as the closing session.
3. Write the **seven big-idea sentences**, one per movement, on a single index card. That card is your permanent teaching skeleton.

---

## 8. FIELD NOTES & RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

- **Teaching is the deepest study.** Nothing exposes a hole in your understanding like a man asking the question you skipped. Your men's study is live-fire — use it every week (2 Tim 2:2).
- **Pray the passage before you parse it.** Study without communion produces a Pharisee. Open each session asking the Author to open the text.
- **Stay slow.** If a week's sector isn't finished, repeat it. The calendar serves the depth, not the reverse.
- **Guard the Sabbath.** Rest is part of the campaign, not a gap in it. Cease, and let God carry the harvest you cannot manufacture.
